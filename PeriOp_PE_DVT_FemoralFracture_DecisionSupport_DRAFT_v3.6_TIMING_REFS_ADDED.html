Peri-operative PE/DVT Risk-Balancing Decision Support Tool for Femoral Fractures

Important safety note: Run this tool both pre-operatively and again post-operatively. Decisions should be made jointly by medical and surgical teams where uncertainty exists.
Do NOT use this tool (seek immediate senior / critical care input) if:
  • Haemodynamic instability / suspected high-risk PE requiring emergency resuscitation, thrombolysis, embolectomy, or ECMO-level care.
  • Active major bleeding, uncontrolled coagulopathy, or a need for immediate life-saving surgery where anticoagulation decisions must be bespoke.
  • Clinical picture is not VTE (alternative diagnosis requiring a different emergency pathway).
Surgical timing principle:
Proceed to surgery as early as clinically safe. “48–72 hours” (if referenced) is an operational MDT review trigger, not a biological safety cut-off.
Seek Haematology / Thrombosis advice early if ANY apply:
  • Known/suspected bleeding disorder, unexplained abnormal baseline PT/APTT/fibrinogen, or suspected DIC.
  • High-risk thrombophilia (e.g., antiphospholipid syndrome, recurrent VTE on anticoagulation, severe/combined inherited thrombophilias).
  • Current or previous HIT or suspected HIT.
  • Severe renal failure (eGFR <15 ml/min or dialysis) or advanced liver disease with coagulopathy/thrombocytopenia.
  • Additional critical anticoagulation indications (e.g., mechanical heart valve, recent arterial thrombosis/LV thrombus).
Prerequisites for using this tool:
  • ECG – perform in all patients with suspected or confirmed PE.
  • Cardiac biomarkers – Troponin (± BNP/NT-proBNP) in all patients to assess myocardial injury.
  • Echocardiography – required if PE diagnosis was made by V/Q scan to assess right ventricular function.
  • Lower-limb venous Doppler ultrasound (bilateral) – in the presence of pulmonary embolism, to establish DVT status and anatomy (proximal vs distal), where feasible (do not delay urgent surgery solely to obtain Dopplers unless it will change management).
1) PE severity
ⓘ What qualifies as GREEN / AMBER / RED?
  • GREEN: No PE on imaging, OR confirmed PE with haemodynamic stability, no RV dysfunction on echo/CT, and normal cardiac biomarkers (troponin/BNP).
  • AMBER: Confirmed PE with RV dysfunction on imaging and/or raised biomarkers, BUT haemodynamically stable (no shock/hypotension), and no immediate need for thrombolysis.
  • RED: PE with haemodynamic instability (sustained hypotension, shock, vasopressor requirement, cardiac arrest) or requiring thrombolysis, embolectomy, or ICU-level care.
2) DVT status
ⓘ What qualifies as GREEN / AMBER / RED?
  • GREEN: No proximal DVT detected on standard CUS (distal calf veins not routinely assessed in standard pathways unless whole-leg ultrasound is requested).
  • AMBER: Documented proximal DVT (popliteal/femoral) with interruption expected to be short and early post-op escalation feasible.
  • RED: Documented extensive proximal / iliofemoral DVT, or situation where interruption is expected to be prolonged with high concern for propagation/embolisation during interruption.
  • UNKNOWN: DVT status not yet imaged / anatomy not confirmed.
3) Surgical bleeding risk
ⓘ What qualifies as GREEN / AMBER / RED?
  • GREEN: Expected secure haemostasis; low likelihood of major post-op bleeding or re-operation.
  • AMBER: Moderate bleeding concern; escalation may need step-up dosing or delayed escalation; closer post-op monitoring required.
  • RED: High bleeding risk (e.g., difficult haemostasis, high likelihood of re-operation/ongoing bleeding); therapeutic anticoagulation unlikely to be safe early post-op.
4) Post-op anticoagulation escalation feasibility
ⓘ What qualifies as GREEN / AMBER / RED?
  • GREEN: Staged escalation expected to be feasible early post-op once haemostasis secure (full-dose within 24h is not assumed as default).
  • AMBER: Escalation likely delayed or step-up required; therapeutic dosing may not be feasible until ~24–72h depending on haemostasis/anaesthetic constraints.
  • RED: Therapeutic dosing not expected to be feasible for a prolonged period (commonly >72h). This is an operational trigger for senior MDT mitigation planning.
  • Governance note: This category reflects bleeding and procedural constraints, not thrombotic risk severity, and functions as an operational MDT trigger.

Evidence labels used in this tool
  • Guideline-based – explicit recommendation from international guidance
  • Evidence-informed – supported by studies and guideline principles
  • Consensus / protocol-dependent – common practice; varies by centre
  • Physiology-based inference – biological rationale; not trial-defined
  • Requires specialist input – decision must not be made in isolation
